About

I have always been deeply interested in technology. As a kid, I would tinker with config files for my PC games and change how the games behaved and looked. It was really cool to see how some small changes to the files would result in different game behaviours.

After graduating year 12 I had gained placement into an engineering degree, but decided to take a gap year to work on superyachts in the Mediterranean Sea. I enjoyed it so much that I ended up spending the next decade doing it. A few years ago I decided to return to Australia, realising that staying in the superyacht industry wasn't going to be as fulfilling as I continued to age. Once back, I chose to work as a personal trainer while I figured out what career path to pursue. It was an easy role to jump into, as fitness is also an interest of mine.

While working as a PT, LLMs started to get interesting and I began building small web tools and other things, mainly using them to help turn my ideas into reality. I'd had web endeavours over the years while working on the yachts, including several Shopify stores, but I always had ideas for websites and applications that didn't already exist. I have a knack for thinking up solutions to problems I observe — it's actually a trait common in my personality type, INTP. The LLM became my developer, and I loved it because it let me turn ideas into reality without needing strong programming skills. But having a web application running in a local dev environment is not the same thing as building it in a way that will let millions of people use it. That's how I started learning about the infrastructure behind web applications. I got hooked. There was so much detail and complexity, and to me, the functionality of the applications exists in the infrastructure. Front-end design doesn't really appeal to me, but learning how data flows through a series of services, how it stays secure, and how this can be achieved for the end user quickly and reliably was fascinating.

The thing that really sold me on solutions architecture as a career path is the depth of understanding required. The more I learn, the more I realise there is so much to learn. Continuous learning and problem solving is something I need to stay motivated in a work environment. I'm also looking forward to helping bring new digital solutions into the real world that people will actually get to use and get value from.
