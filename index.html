About

I've been tinkering with technology since I was a kid — I used to open the config files for games like Monster Truck Madness and adjust the physics values, then reload the game to see what changed. In 2006 I built a website to keep friends updated while I was travelling. None of it was serious, but the curiosity was always there.

After school I went to work on superyachts and spent well over a decade doing that. Eventually I came back to Australia and took some time to figure out what came next — got a personal training certificate, worked as a PT, and thought about where I actually wanted to go.

LLMs started getting interesting around that time and I began building small web tools just to see how things worked. That pulled me into understanding the infrastructure behind web applications — how the backend actually runs, how systems are designed, where the interesting trade-offs live. I like problems that require holding a lot of variables at once, and this field has enough depth to keep learning indefinitely.
