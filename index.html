About

I have always been deeply interested in technology. As a kid, I would tinker with config files for my PC games and change how the games behaved and looked. It was really cool to see how some small changes to the files would result in different game behaviours.

After graduating year 12 I had a spot in an engineering degree, but took a gap year to work on superyachts in the Mediterranean Sea. I enjoyed it so much that I decided to spend the next decade doing this. A few years ago I decided to return to Australia, realising that staying in the superyacht industry wasn't going to be as fulfilling as it was in my twenties and early thirties. Once I was back, I got work as a personal trainer while I figured out what career path to pursue. It was an easy role to jump into, and fitness is a general interest of mine.

LLMs started getting interesting around that time and I began building small web tools just to see how things worked, still unsure of what I truly wanted to do. That pulled me into learning more about the infrastructure behind web applications. I got hooked. There was so much detail and complexity, and to me, the functionality of the applications exists in the infrastructure. Front-end design doesn't really appeal to me, but learning how data flows through a series of services, how it stays secure, and how this can be achieved for the end user quickly and reliably was fascinating.

The thing that really sold me on solutions architecture as a career path is the depth of understanding required. The more I learn, the more I realise there is so much to learn. Continuous learning and problem solving is something I need to stay motivated in a work environment. I'm also looking forward to helping bring new digital solutions into the real world that people will actually get to use and get value from.
